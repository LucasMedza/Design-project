A Basic understanding to the animal kingdom

Animals are living organism that feeds on organic matter, typically having specialized sense organs and nervous system and able to respond rapidly to stimuli. There are many different types that fall into different categories. These categories include: birds, mammals, fishes, insects, amphibians and reptiles.


Birds are warm blooded animals that are special because they have feathers, they can fly, they have bills and their feathers regulate body temperature.
Reptiles are air-breathing, cold-blooded vertebrates that have scaly bodies rather than hair or feathers; most reptile species are egg-laying. They include lizards, snakes, turtles, alligators and crocodiles.
Mammals are the class of animals that humans fall under along with other species like cats, dogs and bats. They are mammals because they produce milk that they feed their children, which is the defining factor of if an animal is a mammal or not.
Insects are living creatures that have six legs and three body parts, those being the head, the thorax and the abdomen. This is why spiders and scorpions are not actually insects.
Fishes are a class of aquatic vertebrates. The combination of gills, fins and the fact that they live only in the water make fish different from all other animals.
Amphibians are small vertebrates that need water, or a moist environment, to survive. The species in this group include frogs, toads, salamanders, and newts. All can breathe and absorb water through their very thin skin. They are also cold-blooded.
