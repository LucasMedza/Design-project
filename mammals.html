Mammals

Mammals are the class of animals that humans fall under along with other species like cats, dogs and bats. They are mammals because they produce milk that they feed their children, which is the defining factor of if an animal is a mammal or not.



Felines

Feline, (family Felidae), any of 37 cat species that among others include the domestic cat, lions, tigers, cheetahs, pumas, jaguars, panthers and lynx. Cats are native to almost every region on Earth, with the exception of Australia and Antarctica. They are carnivorous mammals that live in a wide variety of habitats, but they are typically woodland animals.



Canines

Canine, (family Canidae), also called canid, any of 36 living species of foxes, wolves and jackals, and other members of the dog family. Found throughout the world, canines tend to be slender long-legged animals with long muzzles, bushy tails, and erect pointed ears.
